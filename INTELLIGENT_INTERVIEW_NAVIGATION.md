**For our interview:** We need to understand the **profile**, not just the problems.

## Detailed Process: How Should the AI Navigate?

### The AI's Internal Reasoning Process (Not Rigid Steps)

Let me walk through what the AI should be **thinking** at each phase:

---

### üéØ **Phase 1: Opening & Initial Discovery** (Turns 1-5)

**AI's Goals:**
- Establish warm connection
- Get basic info (name, age, gender)
- Understand **why parent came** (main concern)
- Get initial sense of **severity/urgency**

**AI's Reasoning:**
```
Turn 1-2: Basic info
‚Üí "I have name, age. Good foundation."

Turn 3: "What brings you here?"
Parent: "My son doesn't talk."

AI thinks:
- Primary concern: Speech
- Severity unknown yet
- Other concerns? Unknown
- Need to understand: How severe? Just speech or broader pattern?

Turn 4: "Can you tell me more about his speech?"
Parent: "He's 3.5 and only says mama, dada, and 'more'. That's it."

AI thinks:
- Very limited vocabulary at 3.5 (significant delay)
- Need to understand: Comprehension? Non-verbal communication? Social engagement?
- Also need to check: What DOES he do well? (strengths)
- Don't drill too deep yet - might be other concerns parent hasn't mentioned

AI decision: "Get breadth first - are there other concerns?"

Turn 5: "I hear you. Is there anything else you're noticing about his development?"
```

**Key Principle:**
- ‚úÖ **Get the full concern landscape first** before drilling deep into any one area
- ‚úÖ **Listen for severity cues** (parent's emotional tone, specific details)
- ‚ùå **Don't assume speech is the only issue** - check for co-occurring concerns

### üéØ **Phase 2: Breadth Exploration** (Turns 5-12)

**AI's Goals:**
- Identify ALL major concerns parent has
- Get high-level understanding of each
- Assess which concerns are most pressing/severe
- **Crucially: Identify strengths to balance the picture**

**AI's Reasoning:**
```

AI thinks:

- Now I have: Speech + Social (possible pattern forming?)
- Still need: Strengths, any other concerns?

Turn 7: "I understand. Before we go deeper into the challenges, tell me about the things he loves to do. What brings him joy?"

AI thinks:
- Strength: Sustained attention/focus (can play for hours)
- Strength: Memory (knows character names)
- Interest: Trains (strong special interest)
- Clinical note: Sustained focus on preferred activity + limited social + limited speech = need to understand more about flexibility, eye contact, sensory patterns

Turn 8: "That's wonderful that he has that focus! When he plays with trains, does he act out stories or scenarios?"

Parent: "Not really. He mostly lines them up and watches them go back and forth."

AI thinks:
- Concern detail: Repetitive play (lines up trains)
- Less symbolic/imaginative play
- Pattern emerging: Speech delay + social avoidance + repetitive play
- This needs careful exploration, but parent might be sensitive
- Balance: Show I value his interests while gathering clinical info

Turn 9: "He clearly loves those trains. Tell me, what else is he good at? What are his other strengths?"

Parent: "Hmm... he's very good with puzzles. He can do puzzles way above his age level."

AI thinks:
- Strength: Visual-spatial skills (advanced)

- This is important clinical information

```

**Key Principle:**
- ‚úÖ **Strengths are NOT just "being nice"** - they're diagnostic information
- ‚úÖ **Balance problems with abilities** - paint full picture
- ‚úÖ **AI reasons about patterns** - notices when concerns cluster
- ‚úÖ **AI adapts questions** based on what patterns are emerging

### üéØ **Phase 3: Selective Deep Dive** (Turns 12-25)

**This is where AI reasoning is crucial.**

**AI's Strategic Thinking:**
```
AI reflects on what it knows:
- Concerns: Speech delay (severe), social avoidance, repetitive play
- Strengths: Focus, memory, visual-spatial, loves trains
- Pattern: Possible developmental concern (speech-language delay vs broader?)

Strategic questions:
1. How severe is each concern? (Need to prioritize)
2. Which concerns need detailed examples? (The ones that could indicate broader issues)
3. Which concerns are secondary/less urgent? (Can ask briefly)
4. What's missing from the picture? (Emotional connection? Sensory? Family stress?)

AI decides:
- DEEP DIVE: Social interaction (most concerning pattern-wise)
- MODERATE DIVE: Speech details (already have baseline understanding)
- BRIEF CHECK: Behavior/emotions, sensory, daily functioning
- ESSENTIAL: Parent's emotional experience, what they've tried
```

**Example of Intelligent Deep Dive:**

```
Turn 13: "You mentioned he doesn't really play with other kids. Can you paint me a picture - what does it look like when he's around other children?"

Parent: "At the playground, he just goes off on his own. The other kids might call him to play, but he ignores them."

AI thinks:
- Behavioral detail: Actively avoiding or just not interested?
- Need to understand: Does he respond to his name? Eye contact? Share enjoyment?

Turn 14: "When he's playing alone and you call his name, does he look up at you?"

Parent: "Sometimes. If he's really into something, not really."

AI thinks:
- Mixed response: Not fully concerning (typical kids also tune out when engaged)
- Need more data: Eye contact during natural interaction?

Turn 15: "How about when you're not interrupting his play - like at dinner or reading together - does he look at you? Share smiles?"

Parent: "Oh yes, when we read together he loves it. He cuddles up and looks at the pictures with me."

AI thinks:
- IMPORTANT: Warm social connection with parent ‚úì
- Eye contact in natural contexts ‚úì
- Seeks physical comfort ‚úì
- This changes the clinical picture significantly
- Now the social concern is more about peer interaction specifically, not pervasive social difficulty

AI decision:
- Social: More about shyness/preference vs social-communication disorder
- Can move on, less depth needed here
- Shift focus to: Speech details, has parent sought help, what support exists?

Turn 16: "That's really helpful to know. It sounds like he connects warmly with you. Have you talked to his pediatrician about the speech delay?"
```

**Key Principle:**
- ‚úÖ **AI continuously updates its understanding** based on new information
- ‚úÖ **AI adjusts depth dynamically** - if answer is reassuring, don't over-drill
- ‚úÖ **AI reasons about clinical significance** - which details matter most?
- ‚úÖ **AI pivots based on what it learns** - not mechanical checklist

## How AI Decides Depth: The Internal Reasoning Framework

### Not Rules, But Reasoning Questions

**For each concern, the AI asks itself:**

1. **Severity Check:**
   - Is this concern severe/pervasive or mild/situational?
   - Severe ‚Üí Need detailed examples and context
   - Mild ‚Üí Brief exploration is sufficient

2. **Pattern Recognition:**
   - Does this concern cluster with others (e.g., speech + social + repetitive)?
   - Clustering ‚Üí Need to explore relationships between concerns
   - Isolated ‚Üí Can explore more briefly

3. **Clinical Significance:**
   - Is this detail important for understanding the child's profile?
   - Significant ‚Üí Drill deeper
   - Less significant ‚Üí Move on

4. **Parent's Emotional State:**
   - Is parent emotionally stuck on this topic?
   - Stuck ‚Üí Acknowledge, validate, gently pivot
   - Flowing ‚Üí Continue exploration

5. **Information Density:**
   - Is parent giving rich details already?
   - Rich ‚Üí Fewer follow-up questions needed
   - Sparse ‚Üí Need to probe more

6. **Strength Balance:**
   - Am I gathering problems without understanding abilities?
   - Imbalanced ‚Üí Actively seek strengths
   - Balanced ‚Üí Continue

7. **Completeness Check:**
   - Do I have enough to create meaningful video guidelines?
   - Enough ‚Üí Can wrap up
   - Gaps ‚Üí Continue selectively
